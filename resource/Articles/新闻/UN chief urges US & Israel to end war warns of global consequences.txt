UN chief urges US, Israel to end war, warns of global consequences
联合国秘书长敦促美国、以色列结束战争，并警告全球后果
UN Secretary-General① Antonio Guterres② speaks at a press conference in Beirut, Lebanon③, March 14, 2026.
联合国秘书长安东尼奥·古特雷斯于2026年3月14日在黎巴嫩贝鲁特的新闻发布会上发言。[图片/新华社]
BRUSSELS - UN Secretary-General Antonio Guterres on Thursday urged the United States and Israel to end the war, and pressed for diplomacy to replace military action.
布鲁塞尔讯——联合国秘书长安东尼奥·古特雷斯于周四敦促美国和以色列结束战争，并推动以外交手段取代军事行动。
"It's high time to end this war," Guterres said, addressing reporters here during a European Council④ meeting.
古特雷斯在欧洲理事会会议期间向在场记者表示：“现在是时候结束这场战争了。”
He noted that the conflict started by the United States and Israel was risking getting "completely out of control," while "causing immense suffering to civilians."
他指出，由美国和以色列发起的冲突正面临“完全失控”的风险，同时“给平民造成了巨大苦难”。
Guterres also said the war's fallout was spreading far beyond the battlefield, cautioning of "propagation around the global economy that is really dramatic, with potential tragic consequences, especially for the least developed countries."
古特雷斯还表示，战争的影响正远远超出战场范围，他警告称“全球经济受到的冲击极为严重，可能带来灾难性后果，尤其是对最不发达国家而言”。
"It's time for diplomacy to prevail over war," he added.
他补充道：“现在是时候让外交战胜战争了。”
Leaders of the European Union⑤ member states, meeting in Brussels on Thursday, were set to discuss the Middle East escalation, especially its impact on European energy prices, energy security and regional stability.
欧盟成员国领导人于周四在布鲁塞尔召开会议，将讨论中东局势升级问题，尤其是其对欧洲能源价格、能源安全和地区稳定的影响。
The United States and Israel launched massive attacks on Iran⑥ on Feb 28, disrupting global shipping, sending oil prices soaring and shaking the global economy.
美国和以色列于2月28日对伊朗发动大规模袭击，扰乱了全球航运，推高油价并冲击全球经济。
European gas and oil prices rose sharply in early trading on Thursday.
欧洲天然气和石油价格在周四早盘交易中大幅上涨。
The Dutch TTF⑦ benchmark, a key reference for European gas supply contracts, surged more than 30 percent to 70.7 euros (about 76.8 US dollars) per megawatt-hour at the open, before easing to around 67 euros per megawatt-hour.
作为欧洲天然气供应合同的关键参考基准，荷兰TTF基准价格开盘时飙升超30%，达到每兆瓦时70.7欧元（约合76.8美元），随后回落至每兆瓦时约67欧元。
The price has more than doubled from around 32 euros megawatt-hour before the conflict began.
该价格较冲突爆发前的每兆瓦时约32欧元已翻倍有余。
Oil prices also moved higher. Brent crude⑧, the international benchmark, rose to above 116 dollars per barrel in early trading.
油价同样走高。作为国际基准的布伦特原油在早盘交易中升至每桶116美元以上。

EXERCISES

1.Which of the following is NOT a consequence of the US-Israel attacks on Iran mentioned in the article?
A. Disruption of global shipping
B. Surge in European gas and oil prices
C. Improvement of regional stability in the Middle East
D. Shaking of the global economy

2.According to UN Secretary-General Guterres, what are the two main risks posed by the US-Israel initiated conflict?

3. How do you think the escalation of the Middle East conflict affects global energy security, and what role can international organizations like the UN play in mitigating such impacts?

Key to Exercises
1.C
解析：文章明确提到美以袭击伊朗导致全球航运中断、欧洲油气价格飙升、全球经济受冲击，而中东地区稳定是欧盟讨论的受影响问题，并非改善，因此选C。
2.
（1） The conflict is at risk of getting completely out of control and causing immense suffering to civilians.
（2） The war's fallout is spreading to the global economy, bringing dramatic and potential tragic consequences, especially for the least developed countries.
3.（参考思路）：
The escalation of the Middle East conflict directly disrupts global energy supply chains, as the region is a major hub for oil and gas production and transportation. This leads to sharp price hikes in energy commodities, which not only raises living costs for ordinary people but also puts pressure on the economic recovery of countries around the world, especially energy-importing and least developed countries.
International organizations like the UN can play a critical role by: 1) Calling for an immediate ceasefire and promoting diplomatic negotiations to de-escalate tensions; 2) Coordinating global energy supply and demand to stabilize prices; 3) Providing humanitarian assistance to affected civilian populations; 4) Advocating for a long-term peaceful solution to the Middle East issue to eliminate the root cause of energy security risks.
