I hated him.
我讨厌他。
I didn’t plan on it.
我本不想这样。
In fact, I wanted to like S.
事实上，我本想喜欢S。
I wanted him to like me.
我也希望他能喜欢我。

CONTEXT NAME02

Before teaching my first college journalism class at a private university in Virginia, I expected to forge friendships with all my students.
在弗吉尼亚州一所私立大学教授我的第一堂大学新闻课之前，我期待能和所有学生建立友谊。
I’d be that young cool professor, approachable and informal.
我要做那个年轻又酷的教授，平易近人、不拘小节。
I’d have my students call me Eric.
我会让学生们叫我埃里克。
We’d laugh and hang out.
我们会一起欢笑、一起闲逛。
And they’d love my class.
他们会爱上我的课。

Then the first day of the semester arrived, and there it was—this invisible line I wasn’t sure how to cross.
然后学期的第一天到来了，那条无形的界限就摆在眼前，我不确定该如何跨越。
The students were on one side of it.
学生们在界限的一边。
And I was on the other.
而我在另一边。
I tried to chat with a few of them, but it felt forced and awkward.
我试着和其中几个学生聊天，但感觉很刻意、很尴尬。
I had this unnerving feeling that I was a stand-up comedian hoping to win their applause.
我有一种不安的感觉，觉得自己像个单口喜剧演员，盼着能赢得他们的掌声。
In the corner, S. looked as if he was about to fall asleep.
角落里，S看起来快要睡着了。

Each week, I would try new things to engage the students and make the class fun.
每周我都会尝试新方法来吸引学生，让课堂变得有趣。
I created mock news conferences.
我组织模拟新闻发布会。
I took them on a walking tour and had them write about it on deadline.
我带他们去徒步旅行，让他们在截止日期前写下见闻。
But S. rolled his eyes with each new project.
但每个新项目都让S翻起了白眼。
One day I asked the class to write about an important person in their lives, an assignment designed to hone their use of details.
有一天我让全班写一个生命中重要的人，这个作业旨在磨练他们对细节的运用。
S. let out a sigh that echoed across the room.
S叹了口气，叹息声在整个房间里回荡。

He became my nemesis.
他成了我的劲敌。
The night before class, I’d lie in bed thinking about how I had to endure him the next day.
上课前一晚，我躺在床上想着第二天要如何忍受他。
I could see his mop of hair dangling over his eyes, his slouch, his bored look.
我能想象到他那乱蓬蓬的头发垂在眼前、他无精打采的姿态、他一脸厌倦的表情。
I dreaded him.
我很怕他。
Yet somehow I still thought I’d win him over.
但不知为何，我仍觉得能打动他。
Instead, as the weeks rolled by, things got worse.
可随着几周过去，情况变得更糟了。
S.’s disdain for the class and for me grew more open.
S对这门课和对我的鄙视越来越不加掩饰。
He was a virus, and he was contaminating everyone around him!
他就像病毒，在感染身边的每一个人！
A lesson would be flowing, the students enthusiastic, and S. would bring it to a halt with his downer vibes.
一堂课正上得顺畅，学生们热情高涨，S却用他消极的情绪打断一切。

One day in class, I met with the students individually to talk about their projects.
有一天上课，我和学生们单独面谈他们的课题。
The others waited for their turn.
其他人在排队等候。
But S. grew impatient and started to walk out before I called him and asked him to sit.
但S变得不耐烦，在我叫他坐下之前就起身要走。
He did so reluctantly.
他不情愿地坐了下来。
His e-mails to me began to get ruder.
他发给我的邮件也开始变得越来越无礼。

Finally I’d had enough.
最终我忍无可忍了。
I called him to my office and told him I wanted him out of the class.
我把他叫到办公室，告诉他我要把他赶出课堂。
I had no other choice.
我别无选择。
He was infecting the other students with his derision, I explained.
我解释说，他的嘲讽正在感染其他学生。
Surprisingly, he seemed to understand.
令人惊讶的是，他似乎理解了。
He didn’t argue or get belligerent.
他没有争辩，也没有变得好斗。
Instead, he nodded and left.
相反，他点了点头，离开了。

After he dropped the course, I felt terrible.
他退课后，我心里很不好受。
And terrific.
却又感到释然。
I wasn’t making friends with my students; I was giving them the boot!
我不是在和学生交朋友，而是在把他们赶走！
Yet I knew this had been the right thing to do.
但我知道这么做是对的。
S. had forced me to take back control of my class.
S逼我重新掌控了课堂。
And in the process, he’d taught me something valuable: Sometimes, you need to be tough to be a good teacher.
在这个过程中，他也教会了我宝贵的一课：有时候，要成为好老师，你必须强硬。
My fantasies about college teaching had been naive.
我对大学教学的幻想太天真了。
My students and I weren’t going to be buddies.
我和学生们成不了哥们。
But I could create and maintain an environment where they could learn.
但我可以创造并维持一个让他们能学习的环境。

I ran into S. months later on campus.
几个月后，我在校园里偶遇了S。
I looked up and saw him heading my way.
我抬头看见他朝我走来。
I wasn’t sure what he’d say or do.
我不确定他会说什么、做什么。
Would he swear at me?
他会骂我吗？
Would he give me the finger?
他会对我竖中指吗？
Instead as we got closer, he smiled.
可当我们走近时，他却笑了。
He was pleasant and asked how I was.
他态度友善，问我过得怎么样。
We didn’t talk about the class.
我们没聊那门课。
Instead he just shook my hand.
他只是和我握了握手。
It was the nicest he’d ever been to me.
这是他对我最友善的一次。
